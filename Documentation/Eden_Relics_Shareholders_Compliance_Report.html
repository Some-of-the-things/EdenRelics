Executive Summary

Overall status: Compliant across all critical areas A full compliance audit was conducted against UK legal requirements for e-commerce businesses. Five critical gaps were identified and all have been remediated. The company now meets its obligations under the Companies Act 2006, UK GDPR, Consumer Rights Act 2015, Consumer Contracts Regulations 2013, E-Commerce Regulations 2002, Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations (PECR), and the Modern Slavery Act 2015. Ongoing attention items remain in web accessibility.

This report is prepared for the shareholders and directors of EDEN RELICS LTD to provide assurance that the Eden Relics e-commerce platform operates in compliance with applicable UK legislation and industry standards. It consolidates findings from security assessments, penetration testing, accessibility audits, and a legal compliance review conducted during the 2025–2026 financial period.

2. Data Protection & Privacy

Requirement: UK GDPR / Data Protection Act 2018 / PECR

The UK General Data Protection Regulation and the Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations impose requirements around lawful data processing, transparency, data subject rights, cookie consent, and breach notification.

Privacy Policy

Status: Compliant (remediated April 2026) The privacy policy was substantially rewritten to meet UK GDPR requirements. It now includes all mandatory elements.
  • Data controller identification — EDEN RELICS LTD, Company No. 17153907, with contact details
  • Lawful bases for processing — contract, legal obligation, legitimate interests, and consent are each specified
  • Third-party data processors — Stripe (payments), Resend (email), Google/Facebook/Apple (social login) are disclosed
  • Data retention periods — 6 years for order data (HMRC), until deletion request for accounts, until unsubscribe for marketing
  • Data subject rights — access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection, and consent withdrawal are all documented
  • Data breach notification — commitment to notify the ICO within 72 hours and affected individuals without undue delay
  • ICO complaint rights — contact details for the Information Commissioner's Office are provided

Cookie Consent (PECR)

Status: Compliant (remediated April 2026) Previously non-compliant. A PECR-compliant cookie consent mechanism has been implemented.

A cookie consent banner is now displayed on first visit, offering users the choice between accepting all cookies or essential cookies only. Third-party scripts (Google, Facebook, Apple sign-in SDKs) are only loaded after the user has consented to non-essential cookies. A dedicated Cookie Policy page is published at /cookie-policy.

Data Subject Rights (Self-Service)

Status: Compliant (remediated April 2026) Self-service data export and account deletion have been added to the account settings page.

Registered users can now exercise their right to data portability by downloading all personal data as a structured JSON file. They can also exercise their right to erasure by permanently deleting their account through a two-step confirmation process. These controls are available under “Your Data” in account settings.

3. Consumer Protection

Requirement: Consumer Rights Act 2015 / Consumer Contracts Regulations 2013

Returns Policy & Cancellation Rights

Status: Compliant A detailed returns policy has been in place since launch, fully compliant with the 14-day cooling-off period required by the Consumer Contracts Regulations 2013.
  • 14-day cancellation right — clearly stated, with instructions on how to exercise it
  • Return conditions — fair and reasonable, reflecting the vintage nature of items
  • Refund timeline — 5 working days after inspection, up to 10 days to appear in account
  • Faulty items — 30-day reporting window, full refund including postage
  • Sale items — same policy as full-price items, statutory rights unaffected

Right-of-Withdrawal at Checkout

Status: Compliant (remediated April 2026) The right to cancel is now summarised directly on the checkout page before order placement.

The Consumer Contracts Regulations require that the right to cancel is communicated before the consumer places an order. A cancellation summary is now displayed in the checkout sidebar, including the 14-day period and instructions for exercising the right.

Pricing

Status: Compliant All prices are shown in pounds sterling and clearly displayed before checkout.

Terms & Conditions

Status: Compliant (remediated April 2026) A comprehensive Terms & Conditions page has been published.

The Terms & Conditions cover: company identification, products and pricing, orders and payment processing (including Stripe as processor), delivery options and timescales, cancellation and returns, consumer rights under the Consumer Rights Act 2015, website acceptable use, intellectual property, limitation of liability, data protection, dispute resolution (including ADR and the EU/UK ODR platform), and contact information. The checkout now requires agreement to both the Terms & Conditions and the Returns Policy.

4. Security & Payment Compliance

Requirement: PCI DSS / General Security Best Practice

Status: Secure Two independent security assessments have been conducted. All identified vulnerabilities have been remediated and verified.

Key Security Controls in Place

  • Payments via Stripe — no full card details are stored on Eden Relics servers; only last four digits retained for display
  • HTTPS enforced — HSTS with 1-year max-age and includeSubDomains; all data encrypted in transit
  • Security headers — Content-Security-Policy, X-Content-Type-Options, X-Frame-Options (DENY), Referrer-Policy, and Permissions-Policy are all configured
  • Password security — passwords hashed using ASP.NET Identity (PBKDF2); no plaintext storage
  • Two-factor authentication — TOTP-based MFA available for all accounts
  • Passkey support — WebAuthn/FIDO2 passwordless authentication available
  • Rate limiting — applied to authentication and contact endpoints to prevent brute-force attacks
  • Input validation — all user inputs validated server-side; parameterised database queries prevent SQL injection
  • Social login verification — Google, Facebook, and Apple tokens verified server-side before account creation

5. Accessibility

Standard: WCAG 2.1 Level AA / Equality Act 2010

Status: Substantially compliant with ongoing improvements An independent accessibility audit was conducted against WCAG 2.1 Level AA. Automated scanning returned zero errors across all pages tested. A small number of manual review findings remain open.

Strengths

  • Skip navigation — keyboard shortcut to bypass the menu on every page
  • Semantic HTML — proper headings, landmarks, and ARIA roles throughout
  • Image alt text — all product and decorative images have descriptive alternatives
  • Keyboard focus indicators — visible focus outlines on all interactive elements
  • Form labels and autocomplete — all fields properly labelled with browser autofill support
  • Unique page titles — each page has a descriptive title for tab identification
  • Published accessibility report — available at /accessibility-report.html

9. Recommendations

  1. Legal review. Engage a solicitor to review the Terms & Conditions and Privacy Policy to confirm they are fully appropriate for the business.
  2. Accessibility remediation. Address the five outstanding WCAG items identified in the accessibility audit, prioritising modal focus trapping and screen reader announcements.
  3. Data protection contact. Consider formally appointing a data protection lead or registering with the ICO as a data controller (if not already done).
  4. Annual policy review. Schedule at least annual reviews of all published policies to ensure ongoing compliance with evolving legislation.
  5. Cookie preference management. Add a persistent UI control allowing users to change their cookie preferences without clearing browser storage.
  6. Ongoing security testing. Continue periodic penetration testing and dependency auditing, particularly before and after major releases.
