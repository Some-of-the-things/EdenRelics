## 2. What Eden Relics does (and does not do)

2.1 Eden Relics provides a **curated marketplace**: it vets sellers, reviews and approves listings, hosts your Listings, markets the Marketplace, and facilitates buyer payments through [PAYMENT PROVIDER] (Stripe Connect).

2.2 **Eden Relics is not the seller of your goods.** The contract of sale is between you and the Buyer. You are the seller of record and the merchant responsible for the goods.

2.3 Eden Relics does not take title to your items, does not store or ship them, and does not guarantee any level of sales.

## 3. Eligibility, curation and approval

3.1 To sell, you must be at least 18, legally able to enter this Agreement, and (if a business) properly constituted. You must provide accurate identity, contact, bank and tax information as required for onboarding and for [PAYMENT PROVIDER] verification.

3.2 **Curation is at Eden Relics' sole discretion.** Eden Relics may, at any time and without giving reasons, and without liability to you:
- approve or reject your application to sell;
- approve, reject, edit-request, delay, re-categorise or remove any Listing;
- decline to feature or promote any Listing;
- require additional information, photographs or evidence of authenticity, provenance or condition; and
- set and change curation standards, quality thresholds and house style.

3.3 Eden Relics being a *curated* marketplace does **not** make it the seller, the authenticator of record, or a guarantor of your items. Curation is editorial and quality control; it does not transfer your legal responsibilities to the Platform.

> **⚠️ Review note:** There is tension between "we curate/approve everything" and "we are only a facilitator". The more the Platform vets and approves, the more a court/regulator/consumer may argue the Platform assumed responsibility. The solicitor must confirm the curation language does not inadvertently make Eden Relics a principal, a co-seller, or a warrantor of authenticity, while still allowing tight editorial control.

## 4. Listing standards, accuracy and authenticity warranties

4.1 You are responsible for the content and accuracy of your Listings. Each Listing must include accurate measurements, an honest condition description (disclosing all flaws, repairs, alterations and odours), accurate material/fibre information where known, and clear photographs of the actual item.

4.2 You **warrant** for every item you list that:
- (a) you own the item or are fully authorised to sell it, and it is free of any third-party claim;
- (b) the item is **genuine** and, where a brand or maker is stated, is authentic and not a counterfeit or unauthorised replica;
- (c) the description, era/date attribution, condition and measurements are accurate and not misleading, and any uncertainty (e.g. dating) is honestly flagged;
- (d) the item is not a prohibited or restricted item and complies with `acceptable-use-and-prohibited-items.md`, including product-safety, hygiene, and materials-restriction (e.g. real fur, ivory/CITES) rules;
- (e) the item is fit to be sold and, where second-hand clothing, has been cleaned and meets the hygiene standard in the prohibited-items policy; and
- (f) the sale of the item and your business comply with all applicable laws.

4.3 **Vintage dating.** You must describe era/date/attribution as an honest, informed estimate and must not present uncertain dating as guaranteed provenance. You must not describe a modern reproduction as vintage, or an unbranded item as a specific brand, unless it genuinely is.

4.4 You must keep Listings up to date and remove or mark as sold any item no longer available. Because items are **one-of-one**, you must not list the same physical item on the Marketplace and elsewhere in a way that risks double-selling; if an item sells elsewhere, remove it from the Marketplace immediately.

## 5. Prohibited and restricted items

5.1 You must not list, sell or ship anything prohibited or restricted under `acceptable-use-and-prohibited-items.md`. That policy is incorporated into this Agreement and covers, among other things, counterfeits and IP-infringing goods, real fur and ivory and other CITES/Ivory Act 2018-restricted materials, recalled or unsafe goods, and hygiene requirements for used clothing.

5.2 Breach of the prohibited-items policy is a material breach of this Agreement and may lead to immediate removal of Listings, suspension or termination, and any legally required reporting.

## 8. Refunds, chargebacks and buyer protection — Seller's liability

8.1 As the seller of record, **you bear the cost and liability of refunds, returns and chargebacks** relating to your items, including statutory cancellations, faulty/not-as-described claims, and card chargebacks.

8.2 Where a refund is due to a Buyer (whether under the 14-day cancellation right, for a faulty/not-as-described item, or otherwise), you authorise Eden Relics to make that refund through [PAYMENT PROVIDER], ordinarily by **reversing the corresponding transfer** to your connected account (in whole or in part, including the delivery element where applicable) and/or recovering the amount from your balance or future payouts.

8.3 If your connected-account balance is insufficient to cover a refund, chargeback, reversal, fee or other amount you owe, you must pay Eden Relics the shortfall on demand, and you authorise Eden Relics to recover it from future payouts.

8.4 Eden Relics may apply a **reserve or holding period** on payouts (for example, releasing funds only after the Buyer's cancellation window has closed, or holding a rolling reserve against chargeback risk), at its discretion, to protect Buyers and the Platform.

8.5 You must handle Buyer returns for your items promptly, provide a return address when required, and process approved refunds without undue delay in line with `returns-and-refunds-policy.md`.

> **⚠️ Review note:** Payout holds and rolling reserves are common but are contractually sensitive and interact with Stripe's own rules. The solicitor should confirm the reserve/reversal/set-off mechanics are enforceable and consistent with Stripe Connect's terms, and that Sellers are given fair notice.

## 9. Your consumer-law obligations to Buyers

9.1 As the seller of record you must comply with all consumer-protection law applicable to your sales, including the Consumer Rights Act 2015 (satisfactory quality, fit for purpose, as described), the Consumer Contracts (Information, Cancellation and Additional Charges) Regulations 2013 (pre-contract information and the 14-day cancellation right), and the Consumer Protection from Unfair Trading Regulations 2008 (no misleading descriptions).

9.2 You must honour Buyers' statutory cancellation, rejection, refund and repair/replacement rights (noting that replacement is usually impossible for one-of-one items), dispatch within the stated/lawful timeframe, and describe items truthfully.

9.3 You must not attempt to exclude or limit Buyers' non-excludable statutory rights.
