## 2. Our role — mediation as facilitator

2.1 Because the sale is between Buyer and Seller, most item disputes (condition, description, dating/authenticity, delivery, returns) are ultimately **between the Buyer and the Seller**. As facilitator, Eden Relics will:
- pass information between Buyer and Seller and help them communicate;
- explain the relevant rights (statutory cancellation, faulty/not-as-described, returns);
- help arrange returns and, where a refund is agreed or due, operate the refund via [PAYMENT PROVIDER]; and
- where appropriate, make a **facilitator's assessment** to help resolve the matter fairly.

2.2 Eden Relics' assessment is **not** a binding legal determination or arbitration. It does not remove either party's legal rights, and either party may still pursue their legal remedies.

2.3 Eden Relics may take action under the `seller-agreement.md` and `acceptable-use-and-prohibited-items.md` — for example removing Listings, suspending Sellers, or reversing transfers — where a Seller is at fault or in breach.

> **⚠️ Review note:** Be careful that describing Eden Relics as making an "assessment" or deciding outcomes doesn't inadvertently make the Platform look like the seller or a decision-making principal (undermining the agent model) or create liability for the outcome. The solicitor should confirm the mediation wording keeps the Platform as a neutral facilitator while still allowing it to enforce the Seller Agreement and protect Buyers.

## 3. Faulty / not-as-described and authenticity disputes

3.1 If a Buyer says an item is faulty, not as described, or wrongly attributed/dated, we will help gather evidence (photos, the Listing, condition notes) and put it to the Seller. Remember that **vintage dating is an honest estimate, not a guarantee** (see `buyer-terms-of-sale.md` clause 9) — but genuine misdescription or a counterfeit is a breach that engages the Buyer's rights.

3.2 If the Buyer is entitled to a refund (statutory rejection, cancellation, or agreed resolution) and the Seller does not fund it, Eden Relics may recover the amount from the Seller via the payout-reversal and set-off mechanisms in `seller-agreement.md` clause 8 to ensure the Buyer receives the refund they are owed.

> **⚠️ Review note:** Decide and document what happens when a Seller **cannot** fund a legally-owed refund (insolvent/absent/empty balance). See the buyer-protection backstop review note in `returns-and-refunds-policy.md` Section 6 — this is a real financial-exposure and consumer-protection decision for the solicitor and accountant.

## 5. Chargebacks

5.1 If you paid by card, you may have **chargeback** rights (and possibly **Section 75**, Consumer Credit Act 1974, for eligible credit-card purchases) through your card provider. We ask that you contact us first, because a chargeback raised before we've had a chance to resolve things can slow the outcome and may be unnecessary if a refund is already due.

5.2 Where a chargeback is raised, Eden Relics will respond via [PAYMENT PROVIDER]. As set out in the `seller-agreement.md`, the **Seller bears the liability** for chargebacks relating to their items, and Eden Relics may reverse the corresponding transfer or recover the amount from the Seller.
