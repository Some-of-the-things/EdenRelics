# Commission and Fees Schedule

**Last updated: 13 July 2026 (draft) · Effective: on launch (to be confirmed)**

> **Model assumption (to be confirmed):** Eden Relics acts as a **disclosed agent / facilitator**. The Buyer pays Eden Relics; Eden Relics transfers the Seller's share (minus commission) to the Seller's Stripe Connect account ("separate charges and transfers"). Eden Relics' income is its **commission**, not the sale price. The VAT treatment below depends on this model being confirmed. See `00-README.md`.

This schedule forms part of the `seller-agreement.md` and applies to all Sellers.

---

## 1. Platform commission

1.1 Eden Relics charges a **platform commission of 15%** of the **item price** for each successful sale. *(Decided 2026-07-13; see `DECISIONS.md`.)*

1.2 Commission is calculated on the **item price only** (not delivery). *(Decision recorded; confirm with the accountant when finalising VAT.)*

1.3 **No listing fee** — Sellers pay only the 15% commission on a successful sale. *(Decided 2026-07-13.)*

1.4 Commission is deducted automatically at the point of sale via the Stripe Connect transfer (Section 4) — you do not pay it separately.

---

## 2. What the commission covers

2.1 The commission is consideration for the services Eden Relics provides, including:
- hosting your Listings on a curated marketplace;
- curation, quality control and brand association;
- marketing and driving Buyer traffic;
- the checkout and **payment-facilitation** infrastructure (Stripe Connect integration);
- order routing, transactional messaging and reconciliation;
- Buyer support and dispute-mediation as facilitator; and
- ongoing platform maintenance.

2.2 The commission does **not** cover your own costs as Seller — for example your postage/packaging, your item sourcing, your taxes, or your consumer-law liabilities (refunds, faulty items), which remain yours (see `seller-agreement.md`).

---

## 3. Stripe / payment-processing fees

3.1 Card and payment processing is provided by **Stripe**. Stripe charges its own processing fees on transactions.

3.2 **Stripe processing fees are borne by Eden Relics** out of its 15% commission — the Seller sees a single clean deduction of only the platform commission, with no separate Stripe charge. *(Decided 2026-07-13: option (a), platform absorbs.)*

> **⚠️ Review note (accountant):** The Stripe-fee treatment affects Seller economics, the worked example below, and how fees appear on statements. Under separate charges and transfers, Stripe's processing fee is normally charged to the platform account; confirm with the accountant how it is borne and disclosed, and keep it consistent with the Seller Agreement.

---

## 4. How the money flows (separate charges and transfers)

4.1 At checkout the Buyer pays the **full amount** (item price + delivery) to Eden Relics via Stripe.

4.2 Eden Relics then **transfers to your connected Stripe account** your share — the item price (and delivery, per clause 1.2) — **minus** the platform commission (and minus Stripe fees if option 3.2(b) applies), plus/minus any adjustments, taxes on commission, refunds or reversals.

4.3 Refunds, chargebacks and reversals reduce or reverse your share as described in `seller-agreement.md` clause 8 and `returns-and-refunds-policy.md` Section 6.

## 6. VAT

6.1 **VAT on the commission.** If Eden Relics is VAT-registered, its **commission** is normally a taxable supply of services and **VAT may be chargeable on the commission**. The commission figure will be stated as inclusive or exclusive of VAT, and VAT-registered Sellers may be able to reclaim it — to be confirmed.

6.2 **VAT on the item sale.** Under the disclosed-agent model, VAT on the **item** is the Seller's concern (if the Seller is VAT-registered), potentially under the **second-hand VAT margin scheme**. Eden Relics does not, under this model, account for VAT on the full sale price.

6.3 **Your tax responsibilities.** You are responsible for your own VAT and other taxes on your sales and must provide any VAT/tax information required.

> **⚠️ Review note (accountant — high priority):** VAT is model-dependent and must be confirmed before launch. The accountant must decide/confirm: (i) whether Eden Relics is/should be VAT-registered; (ii) VAT treatment of the commission and how it is invoiced/shown to Sellers; (iii) how the **second-hand margin scheme** interacts with VAT-registered vs non-registered Sellers; (iv) whether any **deemed-supplier / online-marketplace VAT rules** force Eden Relics to account for VAT on certain sales (especially overseas sellers or imports); and (v) the position for any future **international** sales (import VAT, IOSS/OSS, duties). Sections 1, 4, 5 and 6 must be finalised only after this.

## 7. Payout timing

7.1 **A Seller's share is held until the Buyer's 14-day statutory cancellation window has closed**, then released to the Seller's connected Stripe account (from where Stripe pays out to the Seller's bank on its standard schedule). This protects Buyers by ensuring a statutory refund is always covered. *(Decided 2026-07-13.)* A goodwill return in the **voluntary 30-day** window (days 15–30) occurs after payout and is recovered from the Seller's balance / a rolling reserve — see the ⚠️ note in `DECISIONS.md`.

7.2 Payouts may be delayed if your Stripe account is unverified, flagged, or under review, or where a refund/chargeback/dispute is pending.

> **⚠️ Review note:** Confirm the default payout timing and any reserve/hold policy, and ensure it is consistent with Stripe Connect's terms and fairly disclosed to Sellers. Holding funds until the cancellation window closes materially reduces refund risk but affects Seller cash flow — a commercial decision to confirm.
