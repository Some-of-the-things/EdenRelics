## 2. One-of-one stock — no restock

2.1 Every item on the Marketplace is **vintage or second-hand and one-of-one**: a single, unique piece in a single size. There is **no restock**. Once an item is sold, it cannot be re-ordered, and an identical replacement cannot be provided.

2.2 Because stock is unique, adding an item to a basket or wishlist does **not** reserve it. An item is only secured when your order is placed and payment is authorised. It is possible (though we try to prevent it) for an item to sell before your order completes.

2.3 Sizing, measurements and condition vary between individual vintage pieces. You should read the listing's measurements, condition notes and photographs carefully, as vintage sizing rarely matches modern size labels.

## 7. Your right to cancel (14-day distance-selling right)

7.1 Because you buy at a distance (online), you generally have the right to cancel your purchase within **14 days** without giving a reason, under the Consumer Contracts (Information, Cancellation and Additional Charges) Regulations 2013. This is separate from, and in addition to, your rights if an item is faulty (clause 8).

7.2 The 14-day cancellation period runs from the day **after** you (or your nominated recipient) receive the item.

7.3 To cancel, tell us (or the Seller) clearly within the 14 days — for example by email to [SUPPORT EMAIL] or using the model cancellation form. After cancelling, you must return the item to the Seller without undue delay and within 14 days.

7.4 On a valid cancellation you are refunded the price you paid **plus** the basic (cheapest) standard delivery cost the Seller offered. Refunds are handled as set out in `returns-and-refunds-policy.md`.

7.5 **Return postage:** unless the item is faulty or not as described, **you** pay the cost of returning the item to the Seller.

7.6 **Reduction for diminished value:** you may handle the item only as you would in a shop. If its value is reduced because you handled it more than necessary (for example, wearing it out of the house, or removing/altering it), the Seller may reduce your refund to reflect that reduction.

7.7 **Exceptions to the 14-day right.** The cancellation right does **not** apply to certain items, including:
- **Sealed goods not suitable for return for health-protection or hygiene reasons, if unsealed after delivery** (relevant to some clothing accessories/underwear — see the hygiene rules in `acceptable-use-and-prohibited-items.md`);
- goods made to the consumer's specification or clearly personalised (e.g. bespoke alterations).

> **⚠️ Review note:** The hygiene/sealed-goods exception is narrow and often misapplied to clothing. Most vintage garments are **returnable** and the exception only bites for genuinely hygiene-sensitive sealed items once unsealed. The solicitor must confirm exactly which categories (if any) Eden Relics may treat as non-returnable, and how the "sealed" condition is created and communicated, so the Platform does not unlawfully deny statutory cancellation rights. Over-claiming exceptions is a common consumer-law breach.

## 8. Quality and description — used goods (Consumer Rights Act 2015)

8.1 Under the Consumer Rights Act 2015, the item you buy must be:
- of **satisfactory quality**;
- **fit for purpose**; and
- **as described**.

8.2 For **used / vintage** goods, "satisfactory quality" is judged taking into account that the item is second-hand and its stated age, condition and price. Normal, disclosed signs of age and wear (described in the listing) are not faults. However, the item must still match its description and be free of undisclosed defects that make it not of satisfactory quality.

8.3 The **Seller** is responsible to you for these statutory standards, as the seller of the goods. If an item is faulty or not as described, you have the short-term right to reject (usually within 30 days) and other remedies (repair/replacement — noting that a replacement is generally impossible for one-of-one items — price reduction, or refund). See `returns-and-refunds-policy.md`.

8.4 Eden Relics curates listings but does not physically inspect every item and is not the seller of the goods. Where the law nonetheless imposes obligations on the Platform as an online marketplace, we will comply with them.

> **⚠️ Review note:** Because a one-of-one item **cannot be replaced**, the CRA remedy ladder collapses toward repair / price reduction / refund. The solicitor should confirm the correct remedy wording for unique goods and confirm how liability is allocated between Seller and Platform, including any residual Platform liability under online-marketplace consumer rules.

## 9. Authenticity, dating and attribution — vintage disclaimer

9.1 Vintage items are described using the best knowledge of the Seller and the curation process, based on labels, construction, materials and other evidence. **Dating and attribution of vintage garments is inherently uncertain.**

9.2 Where a listing gives an era ("1970s"), a decade, a "St Michael" / brand attribution, or a likely date, this is an **informed estimate, not a guarantee** of exact year, provenance or maker. Genuine experts can and do disagree about the dating of vintage clothing.

9.3 We and our Sellers aim to describe items accurately and to flag uncertainty honestly. If an item is **materially not as described** (for example, described as genuine vintage but in fact a modern reproduction, or a specific labelled brand that is in fact counterfeit), that is a description/quality issue and your rights under clause 8 apply.

9.4 Nothing in this authenticity disclaimer limits your statutory rights where an item is genuinely misdescribed or counterfeit.

> **⚠️ Review note:** There is a line between a *lawful honest-estimate disclaimer* (dating is uncertain) and an *unlawful attempt to contract out of the "as described" right*. You cannot disclaim liability for genuine misdescription. The solicitor must confirm clause 9 stays the right side of that line, and that the CRA and consumer-protection-from-unfair-trading rules are respected. Counterfeits are also an IP and prohibited-items issue — see `acceptable-use-and-prohibited-items.md`.
