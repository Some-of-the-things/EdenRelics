## 1. Two different rights — please read both

There are **two separate sets of rights**, and you may rely on whichever applies:

1. **Changed your mind (14-day cancellation right)** — because you bought online/at a distance, you can usually cancel within 14 days without a reason (Section 3). You normally pay return postage.
2. **Something's wrong (faulty / not as described)** — if the item is faulty or not as described, you have separate rights under the Consumer Rights Act 2015 (Section 4). The Seller pays return postage.

Nothing in this policy reduces your **statutory rights**.

## 2. One-of-one items and how returns work across independent Sellers

2.1 Every item is **unique** and sold by an individual Seller. **Each Seller handles returns for the item(s) you bought from them.** If you buy from more than one Seller in a single order, a return may involve more than one Seller and more than one return address/parcel.

2.2 To start any return or cancellation, contact us at [SUPPORT EMAIL] (or the Seller directly if their details are on your order). We can coordinate with the Seller on your behalf as facilitator. You will be given the correct **return address** for that Seller's item.

2.3 Because items are one-of-one, a like-for-like **replacement is usually not possible**. For faulty/not-as-described items this means your remedy is normally a repair (if you both agree), a price reduction, or a refund, rather than a replacement.

## 3. Cancelling within 14 days (changed your mind)

3.1 You have the right to cancel within **14 days** without giving a reason, under the Consumer Contracts (Information, Cancellation and Additional Charges) Regulations 2013.

3.2 **The 14 days** start the day after you (or your nominated recipient) receive the item.

3.3 **How to cancel:** tell us or the Seller clearly within the 14 days — email [SUPPORT EMAIL], or use the model cancellation wording:
> *"I hereby cancel my contract of sale for the following item: [item / order number], ordered on [date], received on [date]. Name / address."*

3.4 **Returning the item:** after cancelling, send the item back to the Seller's return address **without undue delay and within 14 days** of telling us you're cancelling.

3.5 **Return postage:** you pay the cost of returning the item (unless it is faulty or not as described). We recommend a tracked service and keeping proof of postage — the item is your responsibility until it reaches the Seller.

3.6 **Your refund** will be the price you paid **plus** the basic standard delivery cost the Seller charged on the way out (if you chose a more expensive delivery option, only the basic rate is refunded).

3.7 **Condition / reduced refund:** you may inspect and try on an item as you would in a shop. If the item's value is reduced because you handled or used it more than necessary (e.g. worn out of the home, tags/labels removed, altered, marked or scented), the Seller may reduce your refund to reflect that loss in value.

3.8 **When you get it:** the Seller (via [PAYMENT PROVIDER]) will refund you within **14 days** of getting the item back, or of you providing proof you've sent it back, whichever is sooner.

3.9 **Exceptions.** A few items are excepted from the 14-day right, including **sealed goods that are not suitable for return for health/hygiene reasons once unsealed**, and bespoke/personalised items (e.g. made-to-measure alterations). See `buyer-terms-of-sale.md` clause 7.7.

> **⚠️ Review note:** The hygiene/sealed-goods exception is narrow. The great majority of vintage garments **are** returnable. The solicitor must confirm which items (if any) may be treated as non-returnable and how the "sealed" state is created and disclosed, so statutory cancellation rights are not unlawfully denied.

## 4. Faulty, damaged or not-as-described items (Consumer Rights Act 2015)

4.1 Every item must be of **satisfactory quality**, **fit for purpose**, and **as described**. For **used/vintage** items, satisfactory quality takes into account the item's age, stated condition, disclosed flaws and price — so honestly-disclosed signs of age and wear are not faults. But undisclosed defects, or an item that doesn't match its description (including a wrong brand attribution or a modern reproduction sold as genuine vintage), may give you the rights below.

4.2 **Short-term right to reject.** If the item is faulty or not as described, you can usually reject it and get a **full refund within 30 days** of receiving it.

4.3 **After 30 days.** You may be entitled to a repair or price reduction; because items are one-of-one, replacement is usually not available. If a repair isn't possible or doesn't resolve the issue, you may be entitled to a refund (which may be reduced to reflect use after the first six months).

4.4 **Return postage for faulty/not-as-described items is paid by the Seller** — you should not be out of pocket. Contact [SUPPORT EMAIL] to arrange the return and reimbursement of reasonable return costs.

4.5 If you and the Seller disagree about whether an item is faulty or as described (for example, a disputed dating/authenticity claim), see `complaints-and-disputes.md` for how we help mediate and how to escalate.

> **⚠️ Review note:** For one-of-one goods the CRA remedy hierarchy needs careful wording (replacement is usually impossible). The solicitor should confirm the refund/price-reduction wording and the interaction with the vintage authenticity disclaimer in `buyer-terms-of-sale.md` clause 9 — you cannot disclaim liability for genuine misdescription.

## 5. Hygiene and condition of returned clothing

5.1 Returned garments must come back in the **same condition** you received them: unworn beyond trying on, with any tags/labels attached, clean, free of odours (smoke, perfume, deodorant marks), pet hair and stains, and suitably packaged.

5.2 If a returned item comes back in a worse condition than sent (beyond normal try-on), the Seller may reduce your refund to reflect the loss in value (see 3.7). This does not affect a refund due for a genuinely faulty item.

## 6. How refunds are paid (Stripe / transfer reversal)

6.1 Refunds are made through [PAYMENT PROVIDER] to your **original payment method**. You do not receive cash or bank transfers direct from Sellers.

6.2 Because the Seller was paid via Stripe Connect, an approved refund is normally made by **reversing the corresponding transfer** to the Seller's connected account (in whole or in part) and returning the funds to you. The delivery element is included where the law requires it (see Section 3).

6.3 Refund timing: once a return/cancellation is approved and (where required) the item is received or proof of return provided, the refund is issued within the statutory timeframe (see 3.8). Your bank or card provider may take a few extra days to show the refund.

6.4 If a Seller is unable to fund a refund you are legally owed, Eden Relics will use reasonable efforts (including the payout-reversal and set-off mechanisms in the `seller-agreement.md`) to ensure you receive the refund you are entitled to.

> **⚠️ Review note (buyer-protection backstop):** Decide and document the Platform's position where a Seller cannot or will not fund a refund the Buyer is legally owed (insolvency, disappeared seller, empty balance). Whether the Platform voluntarily backstops such refunds, and how, is a commercial + legal decision with real financial exposure — the solicitor and accountant must confirm. This also interacts with Buyers' card chargeback / Section 75 rights.

## 7. Chargebacks and card protections

7.1 If you paid by card, you may also have **chargeback** rights through your card provider, and possibly **Section 75** (Consumer Credit Act 1974) protection for eligible credit-card purchases. These are separate from this policy. Please contact us first so we can try to resolve the issue quickly.
